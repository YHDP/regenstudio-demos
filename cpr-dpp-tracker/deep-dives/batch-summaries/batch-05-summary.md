### 3. Cross-Family AVCP Mismatches — Growing Pattern
Batch 5 found **three new cross-family AVCP mismatches**, adding to the one found in Batch 4:

| Standard | Families | Issue |
|----------|----------|-------|
| EN 998-1 | CMG vs MAS | CMG: "2+, 4" vs MAS: "4" |
| EN 13813 | CMG vs FLO | CMG: "3, 4" vs FLO: "1, 3, 4" |
| EN 13043 | AGG vs RCP | AGG: "2+, 4" vs RCP: "2+" |
| EN 13055 | AGG vs RCP | AGG: "2+, 4" vs RCP: "2+" |

Pattern: when a standard appears in two families, the AVCP is sometimes incomplete in one. The more specialist family (AGG for aggregates, FLO for flooring) tends to have the more complete AVCP. **Systematic AVCP verification against Commission Decisions is recommended.**

### 4. FLO Spans 8 TCs — Record TC Complexity
FLO draws from CEN/TC 134, 67, 175, 178, 217, 229, 246, and 303 — the most TCs of any family. CMG (4 TCs) was the previous record holder. This complexity will make SReq coordination challenging.

### 5. Two hEN-Only Families
Both RCP and FLO are hEN-only (0 EADs, no Pipeline C). Combined with WCF (Batch 3), this brings the total hEN-only families to 3 out of 19 reviewed. hEN-only families have a simpler regulatory pathway but are entirely dependent on the new-CPR hEN route.

### 6. EN 14041 Standardisation Crisis Gap
FLO's flagship standard EN 14041 has a standardisation crisis gap: the 2018 CEN revision exists but is not OJ-cited. The 2004/AC:2006 version remains the legal reference. This is the first confirmed standardisation crisis gap in the deep-dive series.

### 7. Safety-Critical Infrastructure Families
SBE and CIF are both safety-critical:
- SBE: 100% System 1 (all 14 standards)
- CIF: 80% System 1 (12 of 15 hENs)
These are among the highest System 1 ratios across all 37 families. Vehicle crash testing (EN 1317) and structural bearing testing are among the most demanding physical test regimes in CPR.

### 8. Bridge Infrastructure Domain Overlap
CIF and SBE share the bridge infrastructure domain: CIF covers bridge parapets and expansion joints while SBE covers bridge bearings. Products are correctly classified in separate families but operate on the same structures.

### 9. Empty Content — Reduced Pattern
Batch 5 families have 3–4 empty content sections each (down from 4–5 in Batches 3–4). RCP and FLO have more populated content sections because their standards landscape and development sections were already filled.
