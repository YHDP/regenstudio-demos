**Facade elements (AVCP 2+)** — 5 EADs:
- EAD 090074 (glass-ceramic panels), EAD 090078 (ceramic multilayer slabs), EAD 090095 (fully glazed corner kit), EAD 090101 (cruciform glazing support), EAD 090142 (sintered stone slabs)

**Ventilated wall systems (AVCP 2+)** — 2 EADs:

All 25 EADs are old-regime under CPR 305/2011. ETAs valid until 9 Jan 2036.

### S3. EN 13830:2015+A1:2020 — Fire Propagation as Essential Characteristic, EN 1364-4 Gaps Identified

EN 13830 defines resistance to fire propagation as an essential characteristic — ability of curtain walling to limit fire/smoke spread to adjacent parts. Characteristics include integrity (E), integrity and insulation (EI), and integrity and radiation (EW). EN 1364-4 (fire resistance of partial curtain wall configurations) has been subject to academic critical review identifying gaps: evaluation of fire propagation within the facade system is "not necessarily considered in the fire-resistant spandrel zone." This gap between EN 13830 fire provisions and real-world fire scenarios (highlighted by Grenfell) may drive additional requirements under new CPR essential characteristics. For DPP: fire performance data is already a declared characteristic but may be expanded to include whole-system fire propagation testing data.

**Source**: Intertek EN 13830 (https://www.intertek.com/building/standards/en-13830/); MDPI fire safety research (https://www.mdpi.com/2571-6255/8/8/311); BS EN 13830:2015+A1:2020 (https://www.en-standard.eu/bs-en-13830-2015-a1-2020-curtain-walling-product-standard/)

### S4. Aluminium Circular Economy — 95% Energy Savings from Recycling, 90%+ Recycled Content Facades Built

Aluminium is a circular economy frontrunner: recycling saves 95% of energy vs primary production. Properties maintained regardless of recycling cycles. A German building complex features facades with 90%+ recycled aluminium content. European aluminium industry invested €700 million in new recycling facilities. Post-consumer recycled content in facade systems is increasing: higher post-consumer scrap content yields lower carbon footprint. For DPP: recycled content percentage and post-consumer vs pre-consumer scrap ratio will be critical environmental data. The aluminium facade sector is well-positioned for circular economy DPP requirements.

**Source**: European Aluminium sustainability (https://european-aluminium.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/sustainability-of-aluminium-in-buildings.pdf); European Aluminium circular economy (https://european-aluminium.eu/policy_areas/circular-economy/); GlassOnWeb unitised curtain wall circularity (https://www.glassonweb.com/article/circularity-existing-aluminium-unitised-curtain-wall-facades)

### S5. Post-Grenfell Fire Regulations — BS 8414 Large-Scale Testing, CWCT Standards Influence European Practice

BS 8414 (British Standard for large-scale external cladding fire testing) tests the fire performance of whole cladding systems, not individual elements. Part 1: masonry substrate; Part 2: structural steel frame. First published 2002 by BRE, adopted widely post-Grenfell (2017). Tests performed on full-scale systems incorporating joints, corner details, fixings, insulation, fire breaks, and cavities. CWCT (Centre for Window and Cladding Technology, Bath UK) provides industry testing standards for water tightness, wind resistance, impact resistance, and airtightness. While BS 8414 is a UK standard (not harmonised under CPR), its influence on European facade fire safety regulation is growing — several Member States developing similar large-scale test requirements. For DPP: fire test data from BS 8414 or equivalent European tests may become a DPP-relevant performance declaration.

**Source**: CWCT (https://www.cwct.co.uk/); BS 8414 Designing Buildings (https://www.designingbuildings.co.uk/wiki/BS_8414_Fire_performance_of_external_cladding_systems); Finish Facades (https://www.finishfacades.co.uk/rainscreen-facade-testing/bs-8414-fire-testing/)

### S6. CWP Has Strongest EAD Portfolio — 25 EADs Covering Diverse Cladding Technologies, Transition is Critical Path

CWP's 25 EADs cover an unusually diverse technology range: structural sealant glazing (System 1), mechanically fixed cladding, glued cladding, ventilated cavities, composite panels, ceramic slabs, glass-ceramic panels, sintered stone, sandwich roof panels, and thermal bridge brackets. This diversity means the EAD-to-hEN transition (or new EAD route under CPR 2024/3110) must accommodate fundamentally different material technologies and AVCP systems within a single family. No other Batch 9 family has this EAD complexity. The EAD validity expiry (9 Jan 2031) creates a hard deadline. For DPP: environmental data requirements will vary dramatically across the 25 EAD product types — aluminium curtain walls, ceramic cladding, and composite panels have fundamentally different lifecycle profiles.

**Source**: EOTA EAD database; CWP deep-dive analysis §3
