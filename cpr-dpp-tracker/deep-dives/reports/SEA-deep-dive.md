### S1. European Construction Sealants Market — $4.78B (2024), Silicone Fastest-Growing, MS Polymer Rising

European construction adhesives and sealants market $4.78 billion (2024), projected to $9.39 billion by 2035 (CAGR 5.69%). Sealants segment commands ~43% of the total construction A&S market. Silicone sealants hold largest market share with ~6% growth rate. MS polymer hybrid sealants actively replacing traditional PU and silicone — offering better UV resistance, stronger bonding, and safer emissions. Germany dominates at ~15% of European market. Key players: Sika, Henkel, MAPEI, Soudal, Arkema. The broader European adhesives & sealants market: €19.88 billion (2023), 4.57 million tonnes, forecast to €23.65 billion by 2030 (FEICA data). SEA is a moderate-sized CPR family but shares the broader FEICA ecosystem with ADH.

**Source**: Mordor Intelligence (https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/europe-construction-adhesives-and-sealants-market); Towards Chem & Materials (https://www.towardschemandmaterials.com/insights/europe-construction-adhesives-and-sealants-market); FEICA market reports (https://www.feica.eu/our-industry/market-reports); GlobeNewsWire sealants (https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/10/30/2971527/0/en/At-3-9-CAGR-Sealants-Market-Size-to-Reach-14-17-Billion-By-2031-The-Insight-Partners.html)

### S2. FEICA Model EPDs — 21 Published for Sealant Formulations, Shared Infrastructure with ADH

FEICA's Model EPD system provides 21 published third-party verified EPDs per EN 15804+A2 for adhesive and sealant formulations. Developed jointly by FEICA, IVK, EFCC, and Deutsche Bauchemie since 2016, updated 2022. EPDs are formulation-based (silicone-based, PU-based, etc.) rather than product-specific — any FEICA/IVK/DBC/EFCC member can declare compliance. Sika publishes product-specific EPDs since 2012. Bostik has silicone sealant EPDs. The Model EPD infrastructure directly serves both SEA (sealants) and ADH (adhesives) families — DPP environmental data is largely pre-solved through this collective approach. Key advantage for SMEs: no need for individual LCA studies.

**Source**: FEICA EPDs (https://www.feica.eu/our-projects/epds); Sika LCA and EPDs (https://gbr.sika.com/en/sustainability/sustainable-solutions/lca-and-epd-s.html); IVK/DBC silicone EPD (https://www.klebstoffe.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/Silicone-based-products-group-1.pdf)

### S3. VOC Emissions + REACH + CEN/TC 351 — Compound Regulatory Burden for Sealants

Construction sealants face compound regulation: VOC emission limits (varying by member state — German AgBB/DIBt strictest), REACH SVHC restrictions (isocyanates for PU sealants, phthalates, oligomerisation products), and forthcoming CEN/TC 351 dangerous substances horizontal standards under new CPR M/366. PU sealants specifically face isocyanate exposure controls — shifting market toward MS polymer hybrids with safer emissions profiles. For DPP: sealant declarations will need VOC emission class, SVHC content, indoor air quality data alongside mechanical performance (movement capability, adhesion, cohesion). CEN/TC 351 horizontal methods will standardise how dangerous substance data is generated and declared for DPP.

**Source**: CEN/TC 351 conference (https://www.cencenelec.eu/news-events/events/2023/2023-05-10-cen-tc-351-conference-riga/); REACH substances (https://echa.europa.eu/substances-restricted-under-reach); Low-VOC certification (https://www.wolf-group.com/about-wolf-group/we-save-energy/low-voc-certification/)

### S4. CEN/TC 349 — EN 15651 Editions 2012–2017, No Active Revision, Awaiting SReq 2029

CEN/TC 349 "Sealants for joints in building construction" maintains the EN 15651 series (Parts 1–5). Current editions date from 2012–2017. No specific revision projects found for 2025–2026 — the standards are in a holding pattern awaiting the SReq (targeted 2029). EN 15651 covers non-structural sealants for facades (Part 1), glazing (Part 2), sanitary (Part 3), and pedestrian walkways (Part 4). Part 5 handles AVCP procedures. The SReq under new CPR 2024/3110 will trigger a full revision cycle incorporating environmental sustainability indicators and dangerous substances requirements. SEA has the cleanest TC alignment of any family — all 5 hENs within single TC 349 with no cross-family standards.

**Source**: EN 15651-1 (https://standards.globalspec.com/std/10146513/en-15651-1); CEN construction products (https://www.cencenelec.eu/areas-of-work/cen-sectors/construction/construction-products/); 4ward Testing EN 15651 explained (https://4wardtesting.co.uk/bs-en-1561-sealants-standards-explained-2024/)

### S5. Circular Economy — Silicone Recycling Challenging, PU Recycling Emerging, Cartridge Waste Issue

Silicone sealant recycling is inherently difficult: the extreme stability of the siloxane backbone makes depolymerisation energy-intensive. New universal chemical recycling process (2025) can break any silicone back to single-silicon monomers for infinite reuse — but not yet industrial-scale. PU sealant waste recycling: Sika collaboration project recovers PU waste components for new sealant production. Sealant cartridge waste: millions of used cartridges go to landfill annually — cartridge recycling programmes emerging. Building & construction generates ~40% of EU CO₂ emissions and one-third of waste. For DPP: sealant recyclability is currently low — DPP declarations will need to honestly declare limited end-of-life options, though chemical recycling advances may change this.

**Source**: PMC silicone recycling (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11314909/); ScienceDaily silicone recycling breakthrough (https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/04/250424165447.htm); Sika PU recycling (https://www.sika.com/en/sustainability/news-projects/innovative-collaboration-takes-a-sustainable-leap-in-polyurethan.html); Adhesives Magazine cartridge recycling (https://www.adhesivesmag.com/articles/99155-recycling-silicone-sealant-cartridges)

### S6. Market Trends — MS Polymer Hybrids Replacing PU/Silicone, FEICA Unifies Industry Voice

MS polymer (modified silane) hybrid sealants are the fastest-growing category globally, replacing traditional PU (isocyanate health concerns) and silicone (paintability limitations). MS polymers offer isocyanate-free, silicone-free formulations with good UV resistance and low VOC. FEICA unifies the European adhesives & sealants industry voice across both SEA and ADH families — 800 companies, 90% SME, €19.9B market. FEICA's 2025–2030 market report provides comprehensive forecasting. Soudal (Belgium) is the world's largest independent sealant manufacturer. For DPP: formulation chemistry shifts (PU→MS polymer) will affect both environmental and dangerous substances data declarations.

**Source**: Grand View Research MS polymer (https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/modified-silane-polymer-hybrid-adhesives-sealants-market); FEICA (https://www.feica.eu/our-industry/markets); FEICA market report 2025–2030 (https://www.feica.eu/information-center/latest-news-2/latest-news/european-adhesives-and-sealants-market-2025-2030)
