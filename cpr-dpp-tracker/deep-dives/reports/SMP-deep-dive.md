### New Findings

#### 1. EN 1090-1 Mandate — No Advancement
prEN 1090-1 remains at stage 10.99 following the mandate rejection by EU member states in summer 2025. No revised mandate published as of March 2026. The "early 2026" revised mandate expectation from the initial report has not materialised. This extends the standards development bottleneck — realistic publication now 2029+ at earliest, pushing Pipeline B completion toward 2031–2032.

#### 2. EN 1090-2:2018+A1:2024 Published (Execution Standard)
Amendment A1 to EN 1090-2 published February 2025, adding sustainability-related provisions for execution of steel structures. While not a harmonised product standard (CE marking is via EN 1090-1, not -2), this execution standard update is significant because:
- It introduces sustainability assessment requirements into the execution chain
- Future SReq under new CPR may reference updated execution requirements
- Industry compliance workflows will need to align

#### 3. CEN/TS 1090-201:2024 — Steel Reuse Specification
Published October 2024 (DIN version January 2025). First European technical specification enabling reuse of structural steel components. Scope:
- Complementary provisions to EN 1090-2 for reclaimed structural components
- Covers EXC1, EXC2, EXC3 (quasi-static loading, no fatigue)
- Specifies reusability assessment, mechanical/geometrical property declaration, weldability
- Applies to reclaimed hot-rolled profiles and hollow sections

**Relevance to CPR**: New CPR 2024/3110 has expanded sustainability requirements. CEN/TS 1090-201 is a voluntary technical specification (not harmonised), but it establishes the technical framework for steel reuse that future harmonised standards may incorporate. Directly relevant to DPP content on circular economy and end-of-life data.

#### 4. COM(2025) 726 — EU Steel and Metals Action Plan
Adopted 19 March 2025. Key provisions affecting SMP:
- **Voluntary carbon-intensity label for steel** starting 2025 — precursor to mandatory carbon labels
- **Industrial Decarbonisation Accelerator Act (IDAA)** planned Q4 2025 — may mandate GWP in declarations
- **€100B Industrial Decarbonisation Bank** — financing green steel transition
- **CBAM overlap**: Full financial implementation January 2026 for steel imports. Embedded emissions tracking requirements overlap with DPP environmental performance data
- **Safeguard tariffs**: Steel safeguard measures under review — triple compliance burden (CBAM + ESPR + safeguards)

Strong parallel with RPS findings (iss-RPS-003). Steel families (SMP, RPS) face the densest cross-regulatory environment of any CPR product group.
