### S1. EAE (European Association for ETICS) — Active CPR Engagement, 250M m² Annual Production

The European Association for ETICS (EAE) is the primary industry body for ETICS manufacturers. At the 7th European ETICS Forum 2025 (Milan, November 2025), 200+ specialists discussed circularity, durability, and energy renovation. ETICS are defined as "kits in the sense of the CPR" — prefabricated components applied on-site to facades. EAE confirms the CPR acquis technical process will "provide the foundation for the standardisation request to update existing thermal insulation standards and the future European product standard for ETICS." Annual ETICS production: approximately 250 million square metres across Europe. EPS represents 77% of all ETICS insulation (2024), with Italy, Poland, Romania, and Czech Republic as largest markets. Building renovation accounts for 52% of ETICS applications — directly linked to EPBD renovation targets. EUMEPS (EPS industry voice) also actively engaged on ETICS CPR/circularity issues.

**Source**: EAE (https://www.ea-etics.com/); EAE ETICS Forum 2025 (https://www.ea-etics.com/publications-events/european-etics-forum-2025/); EUMEPS ETICS Forum 2025 (https://eumeps.eu/eumeps-newsroom/news/news-smart-insulation/eumeps-etics-forum-2025); EAE About ETICS (https://www.ea-etics.com/etics/about-etics/)

### S2. ETICS EPDs — Only ~15 Found Despite 798 ETAs Issued

Despite ETICS being one of the most widely used facade systems in Europe, EPD adoption is remarkably low. A systematic review found only ~15 EPDs for ETICS with EPS, XPS, and mineral wool — against 798 issued ETAs. This 50:1 ratio (ETAs to EPDs) represents one of the worst EPD readiness gaps in any CPR family. Challenges: ETICS are multi-component kit systems (insulation + adhesive + mesh + render + fixings), making LCA boundaries complex. AVCP for ETICS sustainability "will be challenging to implement in practice based only on environmental indicators according to EN 15804." Some individual ETICS EPDs exist on EPD International (Berger ETICS EPD S-P-01418). The kit nature of ETICS creates a compound DPP challenge: each component may have its own EPD/DPP, but the system-level declaration must integrate them.

**Source**: MDPI Sustainability 13(24):13705 (https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/13/24/13705); EPD International Berger ETICS (https://www.environdec.com/library/epd1418); EPD International ETICS (https://www.environdec.com/library/epd8931); EOTA EAD 040759 (https://www.eota.eu/download?file=%2F2017%2F17-04-0759%2Ffor+ojeu%2Fead+040759-00-0404_ojeu2021.pdf)

### S3. ETICS Market — €7.7B (2023) Growing to €12.2B by 2031, CAGR 5.9%

The European ETICS system materials market was valued at $7.73 billion (2023), projected to reach $12.24 billion by 2031 (CAGR 5.9%). Building renovation accounts for 52% of applications. The broader European building thermal insulation market has mineral wool as largest segment (42.3% market share, 2024) due to non-combustible properties and EU fire safety regulations. The ETICS market is driven by: EPBD renovation targets (75% of building stock energy-inefficient), EU Climate Law objectives, and national building energy codes. EPS dominates ETICS insulation (77%) but mineral wool growing due to fire safety requirements post-Grenfell. The market scale makes ETICS the commercially most important product category within KAS.

**Source**: Insight Partners Europe ETICS Market (https://www.theinsightpartners.com/reports/europe-etics-system-materials-market); Market Data Forecast EU Building Thermal Insulation (https://www.marketdataforecast.com/market-reports/europe-building-thermal-insulation-market); EUMEPS (https://eumeps.eu/home-insulation)

### S4. Facade Fire Safety Post-Grenfell — EN 13501 and AVCP System 1 Implications

ETICS fire safety is a critical issue post-Grenfell. EN 13501-1 classifies reaction to fire from A1 (non-combustible) to F (highly combustible), with smoke (s1-s3) and droplet (d0-d2) sub-classifications. Post-Grenfell regulations require minimum A2-s1,d0 for buildings above 11m in UK. All KAS ETICS EADs have AVCP System 1 (highest) — requiring third-party factory production control AND product testing by a Notified Body. UK transitioned fully to EN 13501 fire classification from 2 March 2025, replacing BS 476. For DPP: fire safety classification is a safety-critical data field that must be machine-readable and verifiable. The system-level fire performance (not just individual component ratings) determines the overall classification — creating a compound DPP data challenge where component data must be aggregated into system-level declarations.

**Source**: NETZSCH EN 13501 guide (https://analyzing-testing.netzsch.com/en/blog/2025/en-13501-fire-classification-in-europe-and-how-netzsch-supports-compliance); Saint-Gobain Weber fire protection (https://www.de.weber/en/fire-protection-thermally-insulated-facades); Fireproofing Consultancy UK transition (https://fireproof.co.uk/uks-transition-to-european-fire-testing-standards-what-you-need-to-know/); EnviroBuild Euroclass standards (https://www.envirobuild.com/blogs/articles/cladding-fire-regulations)

### S5. Prefabricated/Modular Construction Market — €78B (2025), Timber Leads at 36%

The European prefabricated buildings market is expected to reach $78 billion in 2025, growing to $109.3B by 2030 (CAGR 7%). Modular systems hold 46% revenue share. Timber leads material types with 36.12% market share (2024), driven by embodied-carbon targets. Germany leads regionally (19.2% share) with rapid adoption of industrialised building methods. Volumetric modules achieve up to 95% off-site completion. Growth drivers: tightening embodied-carbon rules, ESG capital flows, chronic labour shortages. CE-marked CLT volumes showed a 12% shortfall in 2024, prompting research into alternative materials. For DPP: prefab units represent the most complex DPP challenge — each module contains dozens of construction products (structural, insulation, fire protection, services), each potentially needing its own DPP, aggregated into a module-level declaration.

### S6. DPP for Kit Systems — Compound Data Architecture Challenge

KAS presents the most complex DPP architecture challenge of any CPR family. "Kits" under CPR are multi-component systems where performance depends on component interaction. Three DPP complexity layers: (1) Component-level: each insulation board, adhesive, mesh, render, fixing needs individual DPP data. (2) System-level: the ETICS or prefab unit performance declaration covers the assembled system. (3) Building-level: EPBD energy performance calculations reference the installed system. The EC feasibility study on CPR DPP (published May 2025) addresses data architecture but specific guidance for kit products is limited. The DPP registry (operational by July 2026) must handle kit-to-component relationships. New EADs under new CPR must include environmental sustainability data: GWP immediately, LCA-EPD core by 2029, full lifecycle by 2031 — this timeline directly affects KAS's 32 EADs.

**Source**: EC DPP feasibility study (https://op.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/cf329d5e-3464-11f0-8a44-01aa75ed71a1/language-en); EC new CPR announcement (https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/news/new-eu-rules-safety-and-sustainability-construction-products-mark-new-step-sectors-competitiveness-2025-01-07_en); BIMobject DPP in construction (https://business.bimobject.com/blog/digital-product-passport-in-the-construction-industry-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-eu-regulation/)
