### E5. Biogenic Carbon Accounting — EN 15804+A2 Mandatory

**Finding**: The biogenic carbon accounting methodology under EN 15804+A2 has significant implications for timber:

1. **EN 15804+A2 became mandatory October 2022** for all new EPDs
2. **CPR 2024/3110 (Jan 2025)** introduces additional requirements aligning with EN 15804
3. **Climate impact split into 4 categories**: total, fossil, biogenic, and land-use change
4. **-1/+1 method**: Biogenic carbon uptake counted as -1 in module A, release as +1 in module C. This "zero-sum" approach over the full lifecycle disadvantages long-service-life timber products compared to immediate-emission products
5. **No consensus on temporary storage methodology**: Despite standards identifying it as important, no agreed method exists
6. **Regional disparities**: European and North American EPDs differ significantly, preventing direct comparison

**Impact**: The biogenic carbon debate is confirmed as ongoing and unresolved. For STP, this is particularly important because: (a) timber products have large biogenic carbon content, (b) the -1/+1 method may understate climate benefits of long-lived construction timber, (c) the CPR's GWP disclosure requirements will need to navigate this methodological challenge. The key_risks content proposal from the initial report correctly identified this. No data update needed but strengthens the risk characterisation.

### S3. CLT Fire Safety — Delamination Risk and National Regulatory Divergences

**Sources**:
- BS EN 1995-1-2:2025 (BSI Knowledge) — URL: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/eurocode-5-design-of-timber-structures-structural-fire-design
- MDPI Fire journal — CLT fire performance review — URL: https://www.mdpi.com/2571-6255/8/10/406
- CROSS-UK Safety Report 966 — URL: https://www.cross-safety.org/uk/safety-information/cross-safety-report/risk-collapse-multi-storey-clt-buildings-during-966
- Arup Fire Safe Design of Mass Timber Buildings (May 2024) — URL: https://www.arup.com/en-us/insights/fire-safe-design-of-mass-timber-buildings/
- JRC Eurocodes workshop (Jun 2025) — URL: https://eurocodes.jrc.ec.europa.eu/2nd-generation/second-generation-eurocodes-what-new

EN 1995-1-2:2025 (second-generation Eurocode 5 fire design) is published with Technical Corrigendum (30 Sep 2025). Major additions: CLT sizing rules, timber-concrete composites, delamination provisions. Coexistence with first generation until 31 Mar 2028, withdrawal 30 Mar 2028.

**Key fire safety issue**: CLT susceptible to delamination at elevated temperatures — adhesive failure exposes inner layers, causing "cyclical burning" rather than protective char buildup. Research shows CLT does not reliably self-extinguish. Charring rate: 0.65 mm/min (no layer fall-off). Arup's "Code Red" research (France) found CLT ceiling fire spread faster than predicted.

National regulatory approaches diverge significantly: UK (CROSS report 966 raised collapse risk concerns), Austria (proHolz guidance positive), France (RE2020 favours timber but fire concerns growing). This fragmentation means DPP fire performance data may need national context.

### S6. FSC/PEFC Digital Traceability — Upstream DPP Data Layer

**Sources**:
- FSC Trace — URL: https://fsc.org/en/fsctrace
- PEFC EUDR DDS — URL: https://pefc.org/eudr
- TimberID (PEFC partner) — URL: https://pefc.org/cms/b999fadb-8521-4d4f-8d3c-4688f57fdced/news/10001145/timberid-trusted-supply-chain-solution-to-ensure-eudr-compliance

**FSC Trace**: Blockchain-based supply chain traceability capturing species, geographic origin, harvest timing, supplier certification, manufacturing transactions. Designed for EUDR alignment. Available to all certificate holders via FSC Connect. Expanding functionality throughout 2026.

**PEFC EUDR DDS**: Dedicated due diligence system with **17+ technology partners** including LiveEO/Orbify/Starling (satellite monitoring), osapiens/Sourcemap (supply chain mapping), TimberID/Timbeter (log identification), IntegrityNext (documentation). No explicit DPP data reuse strategy yet.

**TimberID**: Uses "Dcode" marking on log faces for individual log identification. GPS route tracking, Copernicus satellite imagery analysis, API for ERP integration, TRACES submission. From EUR 100/year for small forest owners.

**STP/DPP connection**: FSC Trace and PEFC EUDR data (species, origin, geolocation, chain-of-custody) maps to DPP sustainability/provenance fields. EUDR creates the upstream data infrastructure that CPR DPP can potentially reuse, but no formal linkage mechanism exists yet. The 17+ tech provider ecosystem is building the digital traceability layer.
