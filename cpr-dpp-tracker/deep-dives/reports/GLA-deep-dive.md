### S2. AGC EPD Generator — Product-Specific Verified EPDs at Configuration Level

AGC Glass Europe launched an EPD Generator integrated into its Glass Configurator tool (end of 2024). The tool calculates 30+ environmental and resource indicators for any configured glazing product, following EN 15804+A2 and EN 17074 methodology. EPDs are third-party verified and available for most architectural glass configurations. This is one of the most advanced DPP-ready digital tools in any CPR family: a manufacturer can generate a product-specific, verified environmental declaration at the point of configuration. Guardian Glass also publishes comprehensive EPDs (float, laminated, coated glass). The Bundesverband Flachglas (German flat glass association) published a 2024 bulletin on EPDs. Tvitec (Spain) and Glas Trösch (Switzerland) EPDs also found on EPD International. The flat glass industry has among the most mature EPD infrastructure of all CPR families.

### S3. Flat Glass Recycling — Only 5% from C&D Recycled, 80%+ Landfilled

Despite glass being infinitely recyclable, only 5% of flat glass from construction and demolition projects is currently recycled. More than 80% of end-of-life flat glass in Europe ends up in landfills. Average cullet (recycled glass) share in new flat glass production has risen from 20% to 26% between 2010–2018. Each 10% increase in cullet use saves 2.5% energy and avoids up to 0.7 tonnes CO₂ per tonne of cullet. Glass for Europe targets closed-loop "flat-to-flat" recycling (not downcycling to packaging). If all end-of-life glazing were recycled into flat glass, cullet share could reach ~40%. Key barrier: no EU obligation to properly dismantle and sort windows/glazing from buildings. Flat glass has more stringent quality constraints than packaging glass. EuRIC joint position paper "From flat to circular" advocates construction glass recycling systems. This circular economy gap is critical for DPP recycled content declarations — the data infrastructure for tracking cullet origin and quality is underdeveloped.

**Source**: Glass for Europe Circular Economy (https://glassforeurope.com/circular-economy/); AGC Recycling (https://www.agc-glass.eu/en/sustainability/decarbonisation/recycling); McKinsey flat glass circularity (https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/metals-and-mining/our-insights/seeing-clearly-decarbonizing-the-flat-glass-industry-with-circularity); EuRIC joint position (https://euric.org/resource-hub/position-papers/joint-position-paper-from-flat-to-circular-rethinking-glass-in-construction); VDMA flat glass recycling (https://www.glassonweb.com/article/vdma-recycling-flat-glass-circular-economy-with-potential)

### S4. EPBD Transparent Building Elements — Glass U-value + g-value Now Required

The European Commission adopted EPBD guidance (30 June 2025) on "transparent building elements" — directly affecting flat glass products. Key development: guidance encourages Member States to set minimum requirements based on both U-value (thermal transmittance) AND g-value (solar factor/solar heat gain), whereas current national requirements are "almost entirely based on U-value." EPBD implementation deadline: 29 May 2026. Article 10 requires new buildings to optimise solar energy generation. This creates a cross-regulatory dimension: glass performance declared under CPR (CE marking, future DPP) must align with EPBD energy performance calculations. DPP data for insulating glass units will need both thermal and solar performance data, linked to EPBD national calculation methodologies. GLA is one of few families at the direct CPR/EPBD intersection.

**Source**: EC EPBD guidance on transparent building elements via Glass for Europe (https://glassforeurope.com/the-european-commission-releases-the-epbd-guidance-on-the-energy-calculation-of-transparent-building-elements/); EC EPBD main page (https://energy.ec.europa.eu/topics/energy-efficiency/energy-performance-buildings/energy-performance-buildings-directive_en); EPBD implementation guide (https://efficientbuildings.eu/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Efficient-Buildings-Europe-Implementation-Guide-2025_online_V3.pdf)

European flat glass market: $21.1 billion (2024), projected to reach $27.9B by 2033 (CAGR 3%). The industry is highly consolidated — top 4 manufacturers (Saint-Gobain, AGC, NSG/Pilkington, Guardian) dominate European production. The consolidated industry structure (unlike fragmented aggregates) means DPP implementation can reach majority of market through 4 companies. All Big-4 already have EPD programmes.

### S6. EN 17074 — Europe's Most Advanced Product-Specific c-PCR for Construction EPDs

EN 17074:2019 "Glass in building — Environmental product declaration — Product category rules for flat glass products" is the most developed product-specific complementary PCR (c-PCR) in any CPR family. It provides detailed rules for EPDs covering all lifecycle stages (cradle-to-grave, modules A1-A3 through C1-C4 and D) specifically tailored for flat glass. A new version is expected 2026, aligned with EN 15804+A2. EN 17074 enables credits in LEED and BREEAM sustainable building schemes. The glass industry's head start on product-specific PCR development means GLA is positioned as one of the most DPP-ready families — environmental data methodology is already standardised and verified. Other families (e.g., CEM with EN 19694, timber with EN 16485) have PCRs but none as mature as EN 17074 for flat glass.
