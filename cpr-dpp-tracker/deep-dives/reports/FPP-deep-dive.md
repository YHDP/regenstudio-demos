### New Findings

#### 1. PFAS Restriction — Broader Proposal Still Evolving
The current PFAS restriction (Regulation (EU) 2025/1988) specifically targets firefighting foams, not fire retardant products in construction. Timeline:
- Firefighting foam ban begins October 2026 (portable extinguishers)
- Full ban by October 2030 (≥1 mg/L sum of all PFAS)
- **Broader PFAS restriction**: ECHA will consult on draft SEAC opinion spring 2026, final opinions expected end of 2026. This broader proposal COULD affect PFAS-containing intumescent coatings and fire retardant products in FPP scope

The impact on FPP remains uncertain but the regulatory direction is clear — eventual broader PFAS restriction is likely. Fire retardant product manufacturers should monitor the broader restriction timeline.

#### 2. EAD/ETA Transition Timeline Confirmed
The transition mechanics are confirmed:
- EADs in OJ by 8 Jan 2026 remain valid until 9 Jan 2031
- ETAs issued under old EADs can be used for CE marking until 2036 (Art. 95(4))
- After 2031: no new ETAs can be issued under old EADs, but existing ETAs continue
- Fire stopping products confirmed as NOT covered by hEN — EAD/ETA is the only CE marking route
- This means from 2031–2036, existing ETA holders can sell but new products cannot enter the market unless new-CPR hENs or new-regime EADs are available

#### 3. 800+ ETAs — Market Scale Confirmed
EOTA confirms over 800 ETAs have been issued for fire protective products. This makes FPP one of the highest-volume ETA markets. The scale of the installed ETA base makes the 2031 EAD expiry particularly consequential — hundreds of manufacturers rely on this route.

#### 4. No New EADs Found
No new EADs for fire protective/stopping/sealing products found in 2025–2026. The 6 existing EADs remain the current set.

#### 5. No CEN Product Standard Activity
CEN/TC 127 continues to develop fire test methods but no movement toward product standard development for fire stopping/sealing products. This confirms the Pipeline A timeline (~2030-2032) is dependent entirely on the 2029 SReq kickoff.

### S1. Fire Stopping Materials Market — $1.7B (2024), Europe >30% Share

The global fire stopping materials market was valued at $1.7 billion (2024), projected CAGR 8.9% to 2034. Europe holds >30% market share (North America >40%). France alone sees 9.3% CAGR driven by stricter fire safety codes. Key players: Hilti, Promat (Etex), 3M, Rockwool, Sika, Fosroc — top 5 hold 20-25% combined share. The market's active consolidation (mergers, acquisitions, facility expansions) and >800 ETAs issued confirm FPP as a commercially significant product family despite its small EAD count (6).

**Source**: Future Market Insights fire stopping market (https://www.futuremarketinsights.com/reports/fire-stopping-materials-market); GM Insights (https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/fire-stopping-materials-market); Grand View Research (https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/fire-stopping-materials-market-report)

### S2. Intumescent Coating EPDs — FPP Has Significantly Better EPD Readiness Than FFF

Unlike fire detection products (zero EPDs), intumescent fire protection coatings have multiple verified EPDs:
- **Nullifire** (Tremco CPG): EPDs for SC802, SC803, SC804 intumescent steel coatings (EPD International, S-P-13759)
- **Sherwin-Williams**: EPD for Firetex M71V2 (NSF verified, EPD10559)
- **Rudolf Hensel**: EPDs for 410KS, 420KS, 421KS per EN 15804+A2 (ISO 14025)
- **Envirograf**: Environmental product declarations for fire protection paints

The intumescent coatings market ($1.36B in 2025, growing to $1.67B by 2030) is led by Sherwin-Williams, PPG, AkzoNobel, Jotun, and Hempel. Hempel launched Hempafire Extreme 550 and Jotun launched Jotachar 1709XT in 2025. Water-based coatings hold 35.3% share (2024) — lower VOC, better sustainability profile. FPP's EPD readiness for intumescent coatings is notably ahead of FFF (fire detection), though penetration seals and linear gap seals likely lack EPDs.

**Source**: EPD International Nullifire SC802/SC803/SC804 (https://www.environdec.com/library/epd13759); NSF Firetex M71V2 (https://info.nsf.org/Certified/Sustain/ProdCert/EPD10559.pdf); Rudolf Hensel EPD (https://www.rudolf-hensel.de/wp-content/uploads/download/EPD_410KS_420KS_421KS_GB.pdf); Sherwin-Williams sustainability (https://industrial.sherwin-williams.com/na/us/en/protective-marine/media-center/articles/sustainable-building-intumescent-coating.html)

### S3. Hilti BIM/Digital Infrastructure — DPP Data Architecture Groundwork

Hilti's firestop division has developed significant digital infrastructure: "Button for Firestop" software automates 3D firestop design by identifying and placing objects into BIM models. "Firestop Documentation Manager" enables jobsite documentation with detailed reports. CFS-CT products are available on NBS Source (UK BIM platform). CAD/BIM Typicals provided in AutoCAD format. This digital product data infrastructure — BIM objects, installation documentation, performance data — closely parallels what a DPP would need. Hilti's system is proprietary, but demonstrates that fire stopping product data can be structured digitally. The transition from proprietary BIM data to standardised DPP would be a smaller leap for Hilti than for manufacturers without digital infrastructure.

**Source**: Hilti Firestop Design Center (https://www.hilti.com/content/hilti/W1/US/en/business/business/engineering/fire-protection.html); NBS Source CFS-CT (https://source.thenbs.com/product/cfs-ct-b-firestop-board/9DohpdSwQGrzP9BuPf9JNy/u2q9uKBReerPT4uroJAwNR)

### S4. Halogenated Flame Retardant Restrictions — ECHA Broader REACH Restriction Pending

Beyond PFAS-specific restrictions, ECHA proposes a "wide and generic restriction" on all aromatic brominated flame retardants under REACH. Further preparatory work is ongoing with restriction dossier development expected after data collection. The broader shift from halogenated to halogen-free flame retardants affects EAD 350865-00-1106 (fire retardant products) directly — product formulations may need to change, requiring new ETAs or EAD amendments. Industry is actively reformulating: epoxy-based coatings hold 34.5% market share due to "robust adhesion and predictable intumescence." The dual pressure of PFAS restriction (foams) and brominated FR restriction (retardants) means FPP faces chemical reformulation risk on two fronts.

**Source**: Pinfa plans for FR regulation (https://www.pinfa.eu/news/plans-for-fr-assessment-and-regulation/); Stellarix halogen-free implementation (https://stellarix.com/insights/blogs/halogen-free-flame-retardants-implementation-complexities/)

### S5. New EAD Environmental Requirements — GWP, Core LCA-EPD, Full List by 2031

Under new CPR 2024/3110, new EADs must include environmental sustainability characteristics with lifecycle assessment indicators on a phased timeline: Global Warming Potential (GWP) upon citation, core LCA-EPD indicators (EN 15804) by 2029, and the full indicator list by 2031. This is significant for FPP because: (1) existing EADs expire 2031 (old regime, no environmental requirements), (2) any new-regime EADs or hENs will require environmental data from day one, (3) intumescent coating manufacturers with existing EPDs (Nullifire, Sherwin-Williams, Hensel) are better positioned, (4) penetration seal and fire stopping manufacturers without EPDs face a data gap.

**Source**: Measurlabs CPR testing guide (https://measurlabs.com/blog/construction-products-regulation-guide-to-compliance-testing/); Fastener+Fixing EOTA route changes (https://fastenerandfixing.com/construction-fixings/what-are-the-changes-in-voluntary-ce-marking-eota-route-under-the-new-cpr/)

### S6. UL-EU Certification and Third-Party Testing Infrastructure

UL Solutions offers a UL-EU Certification Program for firestop products, certifying compliance with EN 1366-3 and EAD 350454-00-1104 (penetration seals). Warringtonfire (Element Group) is a major ETA body for fire protection products. The existing third-party certification infrastructure (UL, Warringtonfire, CSTB) provides a foundation for DPP verification — these bodies already assess product performance and could potentially verify DPP environmental data. The transition from ETA performance assessment to DPP-inclusive assessment (performance + environmental + circular economy data) adds scope but uses existing institutional infrastructure.

**Source**: UL Solutions firestop certification (https://www.ul.com/thecodeauthority/knowledge/product-certification-marks-firestop-systems); Warringtonfire ETA services (https://www.warringtonfire.com/certification-services/fire-certification/european-technical-approvals)
