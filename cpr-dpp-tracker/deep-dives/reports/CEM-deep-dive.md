### E1. CEN/TC 459 Identity Resolution — Iron & Steel, NOT Building Limes

**Finding**: CEN/TC 459 is **ECISS — European Committee for Iron and Steel Standardization** (created 2018). It has nothing to do with building limes. The initial report's references to "CEN/TC 459 (building limes)" in iss-CEM-003 and iss-CEM-007 were based on the tracker's task description, but the actual TC responsible for building limes is CEN/TC 51 itself — whose full title is "Cement and building limes." There is no separate CEN/TC for building limes.

**Impact**: iss-CEM-003 and iss-CEM-007 can be **resolved**. The tracker's `tc: "CEN/TC 51"` is correct. The EN 459-1 `tc_wg: "CEN/TC 51"` is correct. No update needed.

### E2. Alkali-Activated Cement Standardisation — Deepened Understanding

**Finding**: The joint letter (13 May 2025, 19 signatories including Ecostandard, CAN Europe, and industry groups) pushed back against the Commission's April 2025 proposal to downgrade alkali-activated cements from a full standard to a Technical Report. Key details:

1. CEN/TC 51 refused to develop standards for alkali-activated cements, citing "unsubstantiated claims about the maturity of the technologies"
2. The Commission's original February 2025 draft committed to standardising alkali-activated cements; the April 2025 revision backed down to Technical Report only
3. The adopted SReq C(2025) 4828 (28 Jul 2025) includes a Technical Report, not a full standard
4. The EU-funded **Sustacem project** is preparing standardisation of clinker substitutes at CEN's explicit request — technical reports expected mid-2027, ahead of EN 197 update
5. A performance-based standard for alternative low-carbon cements has been requested for delivery by 2030

**Impact**: The existing risk assessment (iss-CEM-001, point 2) is confirmed and deepened. The Sustacem project mid-2027 deliverable is a new data point not in the tracker. The 2030 target for performance-based standard adds to the timeline picture.

### E3. EAD 150080 & Ecocem ACT — Commercial Timeline Confirmed

**Finding**:
1. EAD 150080-00-0301 OJ citation confirmed December 2025 — multiple sources (Ecocem, World Cement, CemNet, S&P Global, Agg-Net) all report this milestone
2. Ecocem's Dunkirk ACT facility (EUR 50M, 300kt/yr capacity) targeting **commercial operation late 2026**
3. ACT technology demonstrates **up to 70% CO2 reduction** vs traditional European cements

### E4. Clean Industrial Deal — Carbon Label Details Confirmed

**Finding**:
1. **CPR confirmed as legislative vehicle**: The Construction Products Regulation will be used to mandate GWP disclosure for cement, making it mandatory in the Declaration of Performance and Conformity for CE marking
2. **GWP metric**: Measured in kgCO2 eq./t, disclosed per product
3. **Timeline**: Originally anticipated end 2025, but the Industrial Decarbonisation Accelerator Act (IDAA) — which would operationalise this — was initially scheduled 10 Dec 2025, postponed twice, expected 25 Feb 2026 per EU Parliament legislative train
4. **Building-level integration**: Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD) requires lifecycle GWP disclosure for buildings >1000m² from 2028, all new buildings from 2030
5. **National precedents**: Germany (VDZ) already operating national cement carbon labelling
6. **CEMBUREAU/Cement Europe position**: Supports "practical and efficient governance model" and lead markets for low-carbon cement

**Impact**: Strengthens iss-CEM-006. The IDAA postponement is new information — the carbon label may come via a separate CPR delegated act rather than through IDAA. Add IDAA reference and EPBD linkage to content.

**Proposed new issue**: Add iss-CEM-008 for IDAA/carbon label timeline tracking.

### S1. GCCA Low Carbon Ratings (LCR) — Global Industry Carbon Classification

**Source**: GCCA, "World's first Low Carbon Ratings system for Cement and Concrete" (24 Apr 2025)
URL: https://gccassociation.org/lcr-cement/
URL: https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250424485662/en/Worlds-first-Low-Carbon-Ratings-system-for-Cement-and-Concrete-launches

The GCCA launched an eight-band Low Carbon Ratings (LCR) system (AA-to-G scale) on 24 April 2025. Numerical definitions first introduced at COP29 (Nov 2024). Uses GWP metrics (kg CO₂e/t). Near-zero threshold: ~240 kg CO₂e/t. IEA CEM I BAT reference: 850 kg CO₂e/t. Designed to work with EPDs.

The GCCA also operates a dedicated EPD Tool (https://gccaepd.org) for generating draft EPDs for clinker, cement, concrete, precast, and aggregates.

**CEM/DPP connection**: LCR provides the global voluntary "carbon class" overlay that will likely feed into DPP metadata. If CPR mandates GWP disclosure on DoPC, GCCA LCR gives industry a recognised classification. The EPD Tool is a major data pipeline for the EPD content that CPR will require.

### S4. CEMBUREAU/Cement Europe Position on Labelling Architecture

**Source**: CEMBUREAU position paper "Unlocking lead markets for low-carbon cement — A labelling proposal by CEMBUREAU" (11 Jun 2025)
URL: https://www.cembureau.eu/library/position-papers/unlocking-lead-markets-for-low-carbon-cement-a-labelling-proposal-by-cembureau/
PDF: https://www.cembureau.eu/media/tbadd5cz/250611-cembureau_position-paper-cement-label.pdf

Two-tier architecture proposed:
1. **EU level**: GWP disclosure (kg CO₂/t) mandated via CPR standardisation request in DoPC
2. **National level**: Classification labels aligned with CEMBUREAU/IEA/GCCA framework

IEA-based near-zero band: 40 kg CO₂/t (0% clinker) to 125 kg CO₂/t (100% clinker). Maximum "low carbon" for 100% clinker: 750 kg CO₂/t. Cement types plotted by GWP vs clinker-to-cement ratio using national EPDs per EN 15804 and EN 16908.

**CEM/DPP connection**: This is the industry's formal proposal for how GWP data flows through the CPR/DPP system. The two-tier model (mandatory EU-level disclosure + voluntary national classification) is the likely architecture.

### S5. EU ETS/CBAM Interaction — Carbon Cost Makes GWP Disclosure Consequential

**Sources**:
- CBAM definitive phase timeline — URL: https://www.coolset.com/academy/cbam-timeline-deadlines-phases-what-to-expect-2026
- Cement Europe EU ETS page — URL: https://www.cementeurope.eu/policy-focus/climate-co2-strategy/eu-ets/
- SCHWENK 2026 concrete seminars — URL: https://www.schwenk.de/en/schwenk-concrete-seminars-2026-transformation-with-realism/

Free allocation phase-out for cement (ETS benchmark: 0.666 t CO₂/t clinker):
- 2026: 97.5% remaining → 2030: 51.5% → 2034: 0%

From 1 Jan 2026, free allocations shift from clinker-based to binder-based benchmark (methodological shift flagged by Cement Europe as unclear). CBAM definitive compliance phase started 1 Jan 2026.

**CEM/DPP connection**: The progressive removal of free ETS allowances (48.5% gone by 2030) makes GWP disclosure on DPPs economically consequential — low-carbon cements gain a real cost advantage. The binder-based benchmark shift must be methodologically compatible with CPR GWP disclosure (which uses EN 15804 methodology). Two carbon accounting systems (ETS and CPR) must align.

### S6. EN 197-5/CEM VI — Not Harmonised, National Approvals Only

**Sources**:
- EN 197-5:2021 on iTeh — URL: https://standards.iteh.ai/catalog/standards/cen/69d3b559-4114-43b3-bfed-05787fc839a2/en-197-5-2021
- Dyckerhoff CEM VI approval (20 Oct 2025) — URL: https://www.dyckerhoff.com/en/w/zulassung-cem-vi-zement
- ALCCC website — URL: https://alliancelccc.com/

EN 197-5:2021 (CEM II/C-M and CEM VI ternary cements) and EN 197-6 (recycled building materials) are **NOT harmonised under CPR** and have NO OJ citation. CEM VI achieves ~340 kg CO₂/t (vs ~700+ for CEM I). Dyckerhoff (Buzzi SpA) received German DIBt approval (abZ) for CEM VI on 20 Oct 2025 — a national technical approval, not CE marking.

ALCCC (Alliance for Low-Carbon Cement & Concrete, founded May 2023 by ECOS) advocates for "performance-based standards" to replace prescriptive standards that disadvantage low-carbon cements. They are on the EU transparency register (ID: 723842295447-39).

**CEM/DPP connection**: Critical gap — innovative low-carbon cements (CEM VI at 340 kg CO₂/t) cannot CE-mark under CPR using EN 197-5 and therefore cannot participate in the harmonised DPP system. They are locked into national approval routes. The prEN 197-1 revision (stage 10.99 since Jul 2025) is the path through which CEM VI could eventually become harmonised, but this is years away. This creates a two-speed system where the most climate-relevant cements are excluded from the EU DPP infrastructure.

### S7. EPBD Delegated Act C(2025) 8723 — Building-Level GWP Demand Driver

**Source**: European Commission, delegated regulation C(2025) 8723 (16 Dec 2025)
URL: https://energy.ec.europa.eu/news/commission-encourages-low-carbon-construction-materials-calculation-framework-life-cycle-global-2025-12-16_en
PDF: https://energy.ec.europa.eu/document/download/a13f9997-fe46-40ec-b158-86149e978624_en?filename=C_2025_8723_1_EN_ACT_part1_v5.pdf

Commission delegated regulation establishing EU framework for national calculation of life-cycle GWP of new buildings:
- **From Jan 2028**: New buildings >1,000 m² must calculate and disclose GWP in energy performance certificates
- **From Jan 2030**: All new buildings
- **By 2027**: Member States must publish roadmaps for potential GWP limit values
- Methodology: EN 15978, 50-year reference, results in kg CO₂e/m² useful floor area
- Data hierarchy favours product-specific EPDs over generic values; Member States must make default data "conservative" so it never outcompetes specific EPDs

**CEM/DPP connection**: This regulation creates the downstream pull for cement DPP data. Building-level GWP calculations need product-level data (cement is a major contributor). Producers with plant-specific digital EPDs will have a market advantage. The data hierarchy explicitly incentivises specific over generic data — driving the same specificity that DPP requires.

### S8. Digital EPD Infrastructure — ILCD+EPD Format and National Databases

**Sources**:
- ILCD XML format explanation — URL: https://epd.guide/standards-and-schemas/ilcd-xml-the-passport-your-lca-data-needs
- ECO Platform Digital Data Requirements v1.1 — URL: https://www.eco-platform.org/files/download/Documents/2025/Digital%20Data%20Requirements_V1.1.pdf
- Construction EPD database landscape — URL: https://epd.guide/epd-data-hubs-explained/the-construction-epd-database-landscape-explained

ILCD+EPD XML v1.3 is the dominant machine-readable format for EU construction EPDs, supporting EN 15804 and ISO 22057 (BIM-ready datasets). InData Working Group harmonising ILCD+EPD with openEPD for cross-Atlantic interoperability.

National EPD databases:
- **INIES (France)**: 5,326 FDES + 1,728 PEP (as of Nov 2025)
- **ÖKOBAUDAT (Germany)**: 2024-I edition (Oct 2024), CSV/XML
- **ECO Portal (pan-EU)**: API access + validation (2025 improvements)
- **IBU (Germany)**: Machine-readable XML for all EPDs

EPD International has 62 fully digital EPDs published as of May 2025 (vs traditional PDF-based). This transition from static PDF → digital machine-readable format is exactly the infrastructure shift needed for DPP.

**CEM/DPP connection**: The digital EPD infrastructure (ILCD+EPD format, national databases, digital EPD programmes) is the existing data layer that CPR's DPP system will build upon. Cement EPDs already exist in these databases. The gap is specificity: current EPDs are per-product-type; DPP may require per-batch or per-plant data. Holcim's Climate Earth automation and Heidelberg's Carbon Bank address this gap.
