### S1. EN 16510 Series Took Effect 9 November 2025 — Largest Harmonisation Event for SHA

The EN 16510 series for residential solid fuel burning appliances took effect on 9 November 2025, replacing five older individual standards: EN 13240 (room heaters), EN 13229 (inset appliances), EN 12815 (cookers), EN 12809 (boilers), and EN 14785 (pellet appliances). EN 16510-1 defines common testing methods; EN 16510-2-x parts establish product-specific requirements. EN 16510-2-7:2025 was harmonised via Implementing Decision (EU) 2026/284 (OJ 9 Feb 2026) with coexistence period ending 9 Feb 2027. Manufacturers must re-test entire product portfolios and update Declarations of Performance. Major manufacturers (HOXTER, Stûv, RIKA) have announced readiness. TÜV SÜD warns industry to "tackle the transition" urgently. This is the single largest harmonised standard transition event for SHA and creates a significant compliance burden.

**Source**: HOXTER EN 16510 readiness (https://www.hoxter.eu/en/blog/we-re-ready-for-the-new-csn-en-16510-standard); RRF EN 16510 series (https://rrf-online.eu/en/the-new-standard-series-en-16510-x2022/); Istituto Giordano requirements (https://www.giordano.it/en/p1-c1271-t1-new-requirements-for-manufacturers-of-stoves-and-solid-fuel-appliances-the-en-16510-standard.php); TÜV SÜD transition (https://www.tuvsud.com/en/press-and-media/2023/march/time-to-tackle-the-transition-to-en-16510)

### S2. CEFACD Industry Crisis Warning — 11,000 SMEs, 200,000 Jobs at Risk

CEFACD (European Committee of Industrial Stove, Fireplace and Barbecue Manufacturers) published a position paper (7 Feb 2025) expressing "grave concerns" about the Ecodesign Regulation 2015/1185 revision: could destroy an industry representing ~11,000 SMEs and 200,000 jobs. CEFACD claims the revision would see "almost 100% of new solid fuel local space heaters disappear from the European market." Critical barriers: no Notified Bodies in Europe currently notified for EN 16510 conformity assessment, the proposed July 2027 compliance date is "completely unrealistic," and re-testing entire product portfolios to comply with both EN 16510 and new Ecodesign limits simultaneously is an unreasonable burden. CEFACD notes biomass accounts for ~59% of EU renewable energy consumption.

**Source**: CEFACD position paper (https://cefacd.com/images/CEFACD_Position_Paper_on_Ecodesign_review_070225.pdf); European Parliament question E-000487/2025 on wood stove ban risk (https://www.europarl.europa.eu/doceo/document/E-10-2025-000487_EN.html)

### S3. Ecodesign Revision Consultation — Stricter Emission Limits Under Review

The European Commission launched consultations on revised Ecodesign and Energy Labelling rules for space and water heaters (28 Nov 2025 – 23 Jan 2026). Draft revision of Regulation (EU) 2015/1185 published 24 Jan 2025. Regulation (EU) 2024/1103 (18 Apr 2024) already updated ecodesign requirements. The revision aims to: set stricter minimum performance thresholds for fossil-fuel heaters, improve requirements for heat pumps/electric/hybrid systems, reflect state-of-the-art test methods for efficiency, sound power, and NOx. This creates a regulatory moving target for SHA: manufacturers face simultaneous EN 16510 CPR compliance AND Ecodesign emission limit tightening — a dual compliance squeeze.

**Source**: EC Ecodesign consultation (https://energy.ec.europa.eu/news/ecodesign-and-energy-labelling-commission-consults-revised-rules-space-and-water-heaters-2025-12-01_en); REHVA consultation notice (https://www.rehva.eu/news/article/european-commission-launches-four-parallel-consultations-on-ecodesign-and-energy-labelling-rules-for-space-and-water-heaters); EUR-Lex draft (https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=PI_COM:Ares(2025)10453861)

### S4. Zero Stove EPDs Found — Industry EPD Readiness Non-Existent

No Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs) per EN 15804 were found for wood stoves, pellet stoves, or any space heating appliance. The heating appliance industry focuses on energy efficiency labelling (A++ to G) and emission certifications (Ecodesign PM/OGC/CO/NOx limits) rather than full lifecycle environmental declarations. DPP under new CPR will require GWP data based on EN 15804+A2 — the stove industry has no EPD infrastructure to generate this. The gap is compounded by the complexity of heating appliance LCA: operational emissions (combustion), fuel supply chain, appliance manufacturing, and end-of-life all contribute to GWP. This is fundamentally different from a structural product EPD.

**Source**: General EPD database searches on EPD International (https://www.environdec.com/services/what-is-epd); EPA wood heater database (https://www.epa.gov/compliance/epa-certified-wood-heater-database) — emissions focus only

### S5. Triple Regulation: CPR + Ecodesign + ESPR Create Unique DPP Complexity

Space heating appliances face an unprecedented regulatory overlay:
1. **CPR 2024/3110**: Construction product performance, CE marking via EN 16510, future DPP
2. **Ecodesign (2015/1185 + revision)**: Energy efficiency, emissions limits, energy labelling (A++ to G)
3. **ESPR 2024/1781**: Potential future DPP for energy-related products

The DPP could be required from BOTH CPR and ESPR tracks. EC has identified discrepancies between CPR and Ecodesign requirements for solid fuel appliances and is "in the process" of resolving them. Construction Products Europe notes this intersection explicitly. The DPP registry (operational Jul 2026) and ESPR DPP requirements (2030 target for all physical goods) create overlapping digital data obligations. SHA is one of the few families sitting squarely at the CPR/ESPR intersection.

**Source**: Circularise ESPR DPP sectors (https://www.circularise.com/blogs/dpps-required-by-eu-legislation-across-sectors); Construction Products Europe SPI position (https://construction-products.eu/publications/sustainable-products-initiative/); White & Case ESPR overview (https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/eight-key-aspects-know-about-eu-ecodesign-sustainable-products-regulation)

### S6. Pellet Stove Market — Europe $430M (2025), 58.9% Global Share

Europe's pellet heating stove market was valued at $430M (2025), holding 58.9% global share. Broader wood pellet heating systems market: $16.35B (2024). Germany dominates with 28.3% European share. Poland emerging with 12% CAGR. EHI (European Heating Industry) publishes annual Heating Market Reports with comprehensive appliance installation data. The market scale means SHA DPP implementation will affect a large, predominantly SME-based manufacturing sector — CEFACD's 11,000 SMEs figure underscores the compliance challenge.

**Source**: EHI Heating Market Report 2024 (https://ehi.eu/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/HMR2024_web.pdf); Market Data Forecast pellet heating (https://www.marketdataforecast.com/market-reports/europe-wood-pellet-heating-systems-market); Fortune Business Insights pellet stove (https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/pellet-heating-stove-market-110683)
