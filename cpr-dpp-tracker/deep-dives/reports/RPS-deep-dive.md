### E1. prEN 10080 — Stage 40.60 Confirmed, No Advancement Found

**Finding**: prEN 10080:2023 remains at stage 40.60 (CEN Enquiry completed/close of voting). No evidence found of advancement to formal vote (stage 50) or publication. The standard is 183 pages and resolves the "framework" problem that caused the original EN 10080:2005 withdrawal — it now specifies technical classes directly rather than delegating to national standards.

Key context from Concrete Centre (UK):
- EN 10080:2005 "had to be withdrawn as a harmonized standard because some countries' legal requirements for additional properties of reinforcement were not met"
- CE marking for reinforcing steel remains **impossible** — "reinforcement cannot be CE marked as the harmonized standard has been withdrawn"
- National standards (BS 4449:2005+A3:2016 in UK, DIN 488 in Germany, etc.) continue to fill the gap

This is the **only CPR product family where the primary hEN has been formally withdrawn** and CE marking is not available. The situation has persisted since 2006 — 20 years.

**Impact**: No data update needed — tracker correctly records EN 10080 as "(new)" with stage 40.60. However, the 20-year inability to CE-mark reinforcing steel is a critical context point that should be documented. The delay risk is severe if prEN 10080 doesn't advance to formal vote in 2026.

### E2. prEN 10138-3 — Stage 40.60, Voting Close Aug 2023

**Finding**: prEN 10138-3 (Prestressing steels — Part 3: Bars) confirmed at stage 40.60 with voting close date August 2023. Note: the tracker may confuse Part 3 (Bars) with the strand standard. The prEN 10138 series has 4 parts:
- prEN 10138-1: General requirements
- prEN 10138-2: Wire (stage 20.60)
- prEN 10138-3: Bars (stage 40.60, Aug 2023)
- prEN 10138-4: Strand (separate, historical)

The full EN 10138 series has never been published — it has been stuck at prEN stage for **20+ years** (earliest versions date to ~2000). Prestressing steel designers still reference national standards (BS 5896:2012, BS 4486:1980).

prEN 10370 (stainless steel reinforcement) also confirmed under development (referenced in BS 6744:2023 revision).

**Impact**: No data update needed. Confirms the extraordinary delay situation. The SReq (Q2 2026) will need to address how to handle standards that have been in development for 2+ decades.

### E3. European Ombudsman Case — Status Unresolved from Public Sources

**Finding**: Case 2145/2018/JAP was opened 22 January 2019 by the European Ombudsman. The complaint concerns "The European Commission's failure to follow-up properly on the mandate M/115 to harmonise standards in construction products concerning Euronorm EN 10080 (on reinforcing and prestressing steel for concrete)."

The Ombudsman's website uses JavaScript rendering that prevented content extraction. The case opening summary is publicly accessible but the decision/closure status could not be confirmed from available web sources. No news articles or press releases were found announcing a resolution.

Key possibilities:
1. The case may have been **closed after the new CPR 2024/3110 was adopted** (Nov 2024) — since the new CPR replaces M/115 with a new SReq process, the Commission could argue the underlying issue has been addressed
2. The case may still be **open**, awaiting the new SReq adoption (Q2 2026)
3. A decision may have been issued but not indexed in search results

**Impact**: The Ombudsman case adds institutional pressure for the Commission to prioritise RPS. With priority 03 status and SReq targeted Q2 2026, the Commission appears to be responding. The tracker correctly notes the case but should document its current status uncertainty.

### E4. Eurocode 2 Second Generation — Published Nov 2023

**Finding**: BS EN 1992-1-1:2023 (Eurocode 2, second generation) was formally published on 30 November 2023.

Key details:
1. Consolidates bridges (previously EN 1992-2) and liquid-retaining structures (previously EN 1992-3) into EN 1992-1-1
2. References EN 10080 and prEN 10138 as normative references — despite neither functioning as harmonised standards
3. National Annex period: 2 years for national calibration + 3 years coexistence = ~5 years total (publication + adoption by ~2028)
4. Steel grades extended to B700 and includes stainless reinforcement
5. In the UK, "in the absence of EN 10080, BS 4449 is used" — this design code / product standard gap persists

**Impact**: The Eurocode 2 / EN 10080 gap is confirmed and significant. Structural engineers design with Eurocode 2 referencing EN 10080, but manufacturers cannot CE-mark to EN 10080 because it's withdrawn. This regulatory paradox directly motivates the high priority (03) for RPS. No data update needed but important context.

### E5. M/115 — Expiry Under New CPR

**Finding**: Mandate M/115 (original 28 May 1997, revised 2009) is the old CPR 305/2011 mandate for reinforcing and prestressing steel. Under the new CPR 2024/3110 framework, old mandates are being replaced by new standardisation requests.

The tracker shows M-III targeted Q4 2025 and SReq targeted Q2 2026. The old M/115 mandate is being replaced by a new SReq under CPR 2024/3110. CEN/TC 459/SC 4 provides inputs to the CPR Technical Acquis subgroup for reinforcing and prestressing steel.

No specific confirmation found that M/115 has been formally expired (unlike CEM's M/114 which expired 31 Dec 2025). The new SReq will supersede it.

**Impact**: Minor clarification. The tracker correctly shows SReq status as "In preparation" for Q2 2026. M/115's formal status (expired or dormant) is ambiguous but practically irrelevant once the new SReq is adopted.

### E6. CBAM & Green Steel DPP — Significant Cross-Regulatory

**Finding**: The Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) has major implications for RPS:

1. **CBAM full financial implementation: January 2026** — importers must buy certificates equal to verified embedded CO2 × EU ETS price
2. **Steel is CBAM's primary target**: Hot-rolled coil, rebar, prestressing strand all in scope
3. **DPP convergence**: CBAM requires embedded emissions data per product — this overlaps with DPP environmental performance data under CPR
4. **Green steel technology shift**: BF-BOF → EAF/DRI pathway; hydrogen DRI for near-zero emissions
5. **Clean Industrial Deal**: Steel is the first sector for carbon label (before cement)
6. **Triple compliance burden**: CBAM carbon costs + ESPR compliance + potential safeguard tariffs

**Impact**: Significant new finding. CBAM creates a parallel embedded emissions tracking requirement that is highly synergistic with DPP. Steel producers implementing CBAM compliance data systems are effectively building DPP infrastructure. This should be documented in content. The tracker doesn't currently capture CBAM.

**Proposed new issue**: Add iss-RPS-003 for CBAM cross-regulatory.

### S1. EUROFER Position — Dual CPR/ESPR Regulatory Burden

**Sources**:
- EUROFER CPR Position Paper (Jul 2022) — URL: https://www.eurofer.eu/assets/publications/position-papers/new-publication-page/20220712_EUROFER-position-paper-on-the-new-CPR.pdf
- EUROFER ESPR Position Paper (Jul 2022) — URL: https://www.eurofer.eu/assets/publications/position-papers/ecodesign-for-sustainable-products-regulation/20220713-Ecodesign-Sustainable-Product-Initiative-position-paper-final.pdf
- Fluxy DPP Guide — URL: https://fluxy.one/post/digital-product-passport-dpp-eu-guide-2025-2030

EUROFER supports CPR/ESPR alignment as "essential for the steel sector" because many steel products have dual use across construction and other applications. Supports minimum environmental performance requirements and harmonised technical specifications.

**ESPR iron & steel delegated act**: Listed as priority product group. First delegated acts (textiles, iron & steel) may be adopted Q3-Q4 2025, with 18+ month preparation window → compliance obligations ~mid-2027. No final draft adopted yet. This creates a **parallel DPP track** for steel: ESPR DPP applies to steel as an intermediate product regardless of CPR hEN status.

**RPS/DPP connection**: RPS faces a unique dual-track situation: ESPR DPP for "iron & steel" products (~2027) AND CPR DPP for "reinforcing steel for concrete" (blocked by missing hEN). The ESPR track may reach RPS products before the CPR track.
