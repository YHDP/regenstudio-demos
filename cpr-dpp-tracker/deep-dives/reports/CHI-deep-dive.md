### New Findings

#### 1. EN 12391-1 Superseded by EN 15287-1:2007 — Strengthens iss-CHI-001
BS EN 12391-1:2003 "Chimneys — Execution standard for metal chimneys — Part 1: Chimneys for non-roomsealed heating appliances" was superseded by EN 15287-1:2007. The word "execution" in the title is critical — this is an installation/execution standard, not a product standard. Execution standards typically do not have Annex ZA and are not harmonised under the CPR. This strongly suggests EN 12391-1 should NOT be listed as cited=true in the tracker.

#### 2. EN 1443 — Confirmed as General Requirements Standard
EN 1443:2019 "Chimneys — General requirements" specifies basic performance criteria and limit values for chimneys. It is a classification/umbrella standard that other chimney product standards reference. General requirements standards typically do not carry Annex ZA for CE marking. Not found in nlfnorm.cz group 6 harmonised listing. Combined with finding #1, the hen_count correction from 18 to 16 is increasingly likely.

#### 3. prEN 1856-1 — Status Remains Uncertain
No new stage information found for prEN 1856-1 beyond what the initial report documented. The genorma "project deleted Mar 2021" entry at stage 40.98 is the latest verifiable data. DIN EN 1856-1 references suggest a draft "prEN 1856-1:2021" was circulated but no formal vote progression is visible. This revision uncertainty remains a key risk for CHI — EN 1856-1 is the flagship metal chimney product standard.

#### 4. SReq Q3 2026 Confirmed — Self-Standing Chimneys Separate
COM(2025) 772 confirms the chimney SReq for adoption in 2026, partially covering the chimney product scope. Self-standing chimneys (EN 13084 series) will be addressed in a separate SReq for "façade fire testing standard(s)" to be adopted in 2027. The separation is confirmed by multiple sources. This creates a two-track timeline for chimney products.

#### 5. Decarbonisation Context
Heat pump adoption across Europe is reducing new-build chimney demand (particularly gas/oil flue applications). However, biomass heating and hybrid systems maintain chimney relevance. No regulatory impact on DPP timeline, but affects market volume projections for DPP system sizing.

### S1. European Chimney Association (ECA) — Standardisation Leadership Since 1992

ECA (European Chimneys Association) is the sole European association of national chimney manufacturers' associations, representing most EU prefabricated chimney manufacturers. ECA experts have been shaping European chimney standards since 1992 and are involved in "nearly every European standardisation project that concerns chimneys and chimney-systems." ECA lists the CPR as a key EU regulation of interest. The association is registered in the EU Transparency Register. Despite this active standardisation role, no public ECA position paper on the new CPR 2024/3110 or DPP requirements was found — suggesting industry engagement on DPP specifics has not yet begun for chimneys.

**Source**: ECA website (https://www.eca-europe.org/); LobbyFacts EU Transparency Register entry (https://lobbyfacts.eu/representative/063353dfbaa74f8c9f7202091fa73298/eca-european-chimney-association)

### S2. ESCHFOE — Workforce and Regulatory Challenges for Chimney Sweeps

The European Federation of Chimney-Sweeps (ESCHFOE), founded 1954, represents national chimney sweep associations. Their September 2024 annual meeting in Prague focused on regulatory/political issues. Most member countries are struggling to attract young workers to the trade. This workforce challenge is relevant to DPP because chimney sweeps perform in-service inspections and maintenance — they are the natural "last mile" data providers for chimney DPP lifecycle data. Workforce shortages could limit DPP data collection capacity for installed chimney products.

**Source**: ESCHFOE website (https://www.eschfoe.com/en/about-us); ESCHFOE 2024 Prague meeting coverage (https://issuu.com/ncsg/docs/sweeping_november_2024/s/60568222)

### S3. Schiedel EPD — First Verified Chimney System EPD Under EN 15804

Schiedel GmbH & Co. KG has published an EPD for the "Absolut Parat" chimney system (double-shell, moisture-resistant, integrated thermal insulation, foamed concrete mantle + isostatically pressed ceramic profile pipes). The EPD was prepared per ISO 14025 and EN 15804, using 2021 production year data, for a 4.7 m chimney at 18 cm diameter. This appears to be one of the first, if not the first, EPD for a chimney system product in Europe. Schiedel's EPD readiness contrasts with the broader chimney industry where no other manufacturer EPDs were found. DPP GWP declarations under new CPR will require this type of data.

**Source**: Schiedel EPD document via Heinze.de (https://www.heinze.de/m2/17/61317/pdf/53/47852553px595x842.pdf)

### S4. Heat Pump Market Shift — Structural Demand Change for Chimney Products

In Germany in 2025, heat pumps outsold gas boilers for the first time: 299,000 heat pumps (+55%) vs 229,000 gas boilers (−36%). EU-wide, air-water heat pump sales grew from 216,000 (2015) to 809,000 (2024) — a 275% increase. The EPBD is pushing fossil fuel boiler phase-out from 2025. This structural market shift directly reduces new-build chimney demand for gas/oil flue applications. However, biomass heating (pellet stoves, wood boilers) and hybrid heating systems maintain chimney relevance. The chimney industry is shifting from gas flue dominance toward: (1) biomass/solid fuel flues with higher temperature and soot fire resistance requirements, (2) condensing appliance flues requiring moisture resistance (W designation per EN 1443), (3) renovation/replacement market for existing buildings.

**Source**: Heatpumpswatch.org comparison (https://heatpumpswatch.org/heating-technologies-compared/); IEA Future of Heat Pumps (https://www.iea.org/reports/the-future-of-heat-pumps/executive-summary); Clean Energy Wire (https://www.cleanenergywire.org/news/rising-heating-costs-2025-impact-homes-gas-boilers-most-analysis)

### S5. Hydrogen-Ready Chimney Standards Gap

Despite the EU hydrogen strategy and growing hydrogen-blend gas network trials across Europe, no specific "hydrogen-ready" chimney or flue standard was found. Hydrogen combustion produces different flue gas characteristics: higher water vapour content, different condensate chemistry, and potentially higher flue gas temperatures depending on blend ratio. Current EN 1443 designation system covers temperature (T class), condensate resistance (W/D), and corrosion resistance (V1/V2/V3/Vm) — but these classifications were designed for natural gas, oil, and solid fuel. A hydrogen or hydrogen-blend flue gas designation may be needed as EU hydrogen deployment accelerates. The BFCMA (British Flue and Chimney Manufacturer's Association) provides detailed guides on chimney designation under EN 1856-1 but makes no reference to hydrogen compatibility.

**Source**: BFCMA regulations and standards (https://www.bfcma.co.uk/regulations-and-standards); BFCMA gas guide (https://feta.co.uk/client/files/BFCMA/1709197774-BFCMA_Gas_Guide_06_02_23.pdf); ChimneyLab.dk testing standards (https://www.chimneylab.dk/engelsk/chimneys/standards-for-chimney-testing)

### S6. EN 1443 Designation System — Confirms Reference Standard Role

EN 1443 specifies a designation string for chimneys based on: Temperature (T class), Pressure (P class), Condensate resistance (W/D), Corrosion resistance (V1/V2/V3/Vm), Soot fire resistance (G/O), and Distance to combustibles (Lxxxxxx). Other product standards (EN 1856-1, EN 1457, EN 13063 etc.) reference EN 1443 for these classification categories. For gas appliances, a metal chimney should meet designation "T250 N2 D V1 Oxx" per EN 1856-1. This classification/umbrella role further strengthens the conclusion from iss-CHI-001 that EN 1443 is not itself an OJ-cited harmonised product standard with Annex ZA, but rather a reference standard that other harmonised standards depend upon.

**Source**: Guild of Master Chimney Sweeps CE designations guide (https://www.guildofmasterchimneysweeps.co.uk/understanding-ce-designations/); BFCMA general guidance (https://www.bfcma.co.uk/siteFiles/resources/docs/BFCMAGeneralGuidance10.12.12.pdf)
