Methodology

VSME alignment methodology — v1

Reporting entity: Regen Studio BV Status: voluntary disclosure; RS BV is not obligated under CSRD Standard reference: EFRAG Voluntary Standard for non-listed SMEs (VSME), EU Commission Recommendation of 30 July 2025 Last update: 2026-04-13

Why this document exists

The Regen CO₂ Footprint Manager publishes an annual organisational carbon-footprint report. This document explains how that report maps to the VSME standard — which Basic and Comprehensive data points it covers, how each number is computed, which assumptions are in play, and where the language is deliberately conservative to avoid claim-risk.

Claim language hygiene

  • The report footer says "structured in alignment with" VSME, never

"prepared in accordance with" — the latter carries assurance weight that RS does not yet support by third-party verification.

  • The report never says "CSRD-compliant." RS is below the post-Omnibus CSRD

thresholds (1,000 employees AND €450M turnover, in force from 18 March 2026, first application FY 2027) and is not subject to CSRD.

  • The report never says "ISSA 5000-assured." It says "assurance-ready" at

most. ISSA 5000 limited assurance becomes effective for periods beginning 15 December 2026; any engagement happens separately.

  • The inline XBRL tags are described as "inline-tagged per EFRAG VSME

taxonomy" — not "validated" (structural validation only; dimensional validation is an out-of-band Arelle run) and not "filed" (nothing is filed).

Boundary and scope

  • Organisational boundary: operational control.
  • Reporting period: full calendar year (YYYY-01-01 → YYYY-12-31).
  • Inclusions: all transactions paid from the business account;

business travel (flights, ground transport, personal vehicle with business-paid fuel); meals paid from the business account; office electricity for the São Paulo home office (3 m², lightweight use); capital goods purchased in the reporting year (full embodied emissions per GHG Protocol Scope 3.2); SaaS subscriptions; printed goods.

  • Exclusions: personal (non-business) travel and expenses; commuting

(N/A for sole-operator home office); any third-party processing emissions attributable to product sold (N/A).

  • Multi-entity note: CircularTech Advisory and DFT Brazil Consulting are

separate reporting entities in the tool's organisations table. This methodology covers RS BV only. Other entities get their own periods and reports.

GHG emissions — GHG Protocol Corporate Standard

  • Scopes used: 1, 2 (location-based + market-based), 3.1, 3.2, 3.6.
  • Dual Scope 2: every Scope 2 activity is recorded twice in the

register — once with scope code 2-location using the grid-average factor for the location, once with scope code 2-market using a market-based factor. The grand total uses the market-based figure per GHG Protocol convention; the location-based figure is shown alongside.

  • Brazil market-based basis: Brazil has no formal AIB-style residual

mix as of 2026. Per GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance, when no supplier- specific contractual instrument applies and no residual mix exists, the location-based grid average is used as the market-based figure. The two BR rows carry identical kWh and kg but different basis markers and different factor slugs so the dual-reporting audit trail is explicit.

  • GWPs: IPCC AR6 100-year Global Warming Potentials throughout. DEFRA

2024 factors already use AR6; other factors are CO₂-dominant so the GWP version is immaterial.

  • Uncertainty: each row carries a 5th–95th percentile range derived

from the factor's value_low / value_high (or lognormal σ_g where specified). Totals are propagated by summation of per-row ranges — this is conservative versus the GHG Protocol Quantitative Uncertainty Guidance's simplified Tier-1 approach which uses RSS; the simpler summation is used here because the tool ships with per-factor ranges rather than per-factor σ.

  • Pietro-trap guard: the dashboard refuses to record a single row

where upper/lower > 100. Named after the 200× ChatGPT figure that triggered the tool's creation.

  • Dates: where a specific receipt date was not retrieved, the row is

placed at a within-period reconstruction date (mid-month or end-of-year as appropriate). Quantities are unaffected; only the intra-year placement is approximate.

  • Aggregated travel rows: NL train (500 pkm) and BR Uber (200 km) are

reported as a single annual row each, summed from real business transactions, rather than per-trip detail. Yvo has explicitly chosen not to publish per-trip travel data.

  • Base-year designation: 2025 is not designated as the base year until

the period is finalized and the methodology is stable. Designating a base year locks the reduction target denominator; restatements are expensive.

Non-GHG disclosures

  • Water, biodiversity, waste: fully modeled and populated even at

near-zero values, because the row structure gives year-on-year comparability for free and matches the DPP consultancy's measure- everything ethos. Small numbers are more credible than a "not material" statement when the values are real.

  • Workforce: 1-FTE entity. VSME Basic requires headcount, contract

type, gender ratio, wages vs minimum, training hours, workplace incidents. For N < 3 cohorts, the GDPR rule kicks in: aggregate or redact. With N = 1 the gender/contract fields are technically re- identifying; the report includes them only because Yvo is publicly identified as the sole operator elsewhere and no new information is disclosed.

  • Governance: anti-corruption convictions and fines are reported even

when zero. VSME requires it; the empty row is itself a disclosure.

  • Materiality assessments: every VSME topic has an explicit material

verdict with a written basis. Amended ESRS (Nov 2025) requires this; VSME permits it. Water, biodiversity, and waste are marked not material with specific bases; climate is material.

Factor provenance

Every emission factor is stored with:

  • slug — stable identifier used in all audit trails.
  • factor_source_id — FK into factor_sources, which carries URL,

publisher, quality rating (A–D per IPCC data quality), and retrieval date.

  • value_mean, value_low, value_high (or geometric_sd for lognormal).
  • geographic_scope, valid_from_year, valid_until_year — immutable

versioning so past reports remain reproducible.

  • calculation_method — one line noting the methodology family.

Factors are versioned INSERT-only. When DEFRA 2025 is released, a new row is added with valid_from_year = 2025; DEFRA 2024 stays unchanged so 2024 and earlier reports remain exactly reproducible.

Every report run writes a calculation_runs row with a SHA-256 over the factor set used. That hash is the assurance-grade receipt: two runs with the same hash demonstrably used the same factors.

Accrual policy

Transactions are placed in the reporting period of occurrence (flight boarding date, invoice date for purchased goods, service delivery date for contracts). A Dec 31 2025 invoice for services delivered in Q1 2026 sits in 2026 by this rule. ISSA 5000 asks for explicit accrual policy; this is it.

iXBRL hybrid approach

The HTML report is inline-tagged per the VSME taxonomy using iXBRL 1.1. Concept IDs come from vendor/vsme-taxonomy/concepts.json, a compact Regen Studio representation of the EFRAG VSME Digital Template taxonomy. The official EFRAG XML taxonomy can be placed under vendor/vsme-taxonomy/official/ as XSD files — the loader prefers XML when present.

A companion Markdown file <org>-<year>-vsme-tagged-points.md lists every tagged data point with concept ID and value so a human can audit what was tagged. This is the "manually monitored" half of the hybrid — diff against the prior year's audit to catch tag-set drift.

Dimensional validation of the emitted iXBRL (full link-base + type checking) is an out-of-band manual step via Arelle. The in-pipeline xbrl.validate_report() does structural validation only — does every ix:* tag reference a concept the catalogue knows about.

Reduction target

  • Base year: 2025 (provisional until final).
  • Target year: 2027.
  • Reduction: −40% versus base year, applied to Scope 1+2+3 total.
  • Mechanism: flight reduction through trip bundling and the planned

2026 emigration from NL to BR (which inverts and reduces intercontinental frequency). Flights dominate the 2025 footprint at ~90%; the lever is identified and concrete.

  • Paris alignment: linear pathway statement. This does not claim

Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) validation — no engagement, not a claim we make.

Carbon credits treatment

RS retired zero carbon credits in 2025. Any future credits go into the carbon_credits table and are reported separately in the Comprehensive section. They are not netted from Scope 1/2/3 totals per GHG Protocol convention; they are disclosed as a separate line. SAF allocation on flight tickets (5% in both 2025 RTs) is treated the same way — as "uncounted offsetting," mentioned in the methodology but not netted.

Internal carbon price

When present, the internal carbon price is reported per period in the Comprehensive section. It is informational — it does not adjust reported Scope totals.

Versioning of this document

This is methodology v1. Any change that could affect the reported numbers requires: 1. A dated entry in methodology/decisions-log.md. 2. A new methodology version increment (v2, v3, …) in this file. 3. A statement in the next report's "Method" block explaining what changed and whether prior periods are restated.
