📖 Glossary

Plain-language definitions of the acronyms and standards used across the footprint manager. Every ⓘ popover links to the relevant entry.

Carbon credit

A tradeable instrument representing one tonne of CO₂-equivalent avoided or removed from the atmosphere. Credits are not netted from Scope 1/2/3 totals — they're disclosed separately. Quality varies wildly; stick to recognised standards (Gold Standard, VCS, UNFCCC CDM).

CSRD — Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive

EU directive requiring large companies to publish sustainability reports using the ESRS standards. Post-Omnibus (Feb 2026), only firms with 1,000+ employees AND €450M+ turnover are in scope. Most SMEs, including Regen Studio BV, are permanently out of scope.

Dual Scope 2 reporting

GHG Protocol rule: when any market-based instrument exists (green tariffs, Guarantees of Origin, residual-mix factors), you must disclose Scope 2 emissions under BOTH a location-based method (physical grid) and a market-based method (supplier choices). The two tell different stories.

Emission factor

A number that converts activity data (km, kWh, tonnes, subscriptions) into kg CO₂-equivalent. Every factor carries a value, a range, a unit, a geographic scope, a valid-from year, and a source with a quality rating (A = peer-reviewed, D = internal judgment).

GHG Protocol

The global accounting standard for corporate greenhouse gas emissions. Defines Scopes 1, 2, 3 and the 15 Scope 3 sub-categories. Memory aid: Scope 1 = what you burn, Scope 2 = what you buy, Scope 3 = everything beyond that.

Intensity ratio

Total emissions divided by a denominator (revenue, FTE, or m²). Makes your footprint comparable across company sizes. Banks and procurement questionnaires especially love kg CO₂e per € revenue.

iXBRL — Inline XBRL

A machine-readable format for disclosure data, embedded inline in the HTML of your report. One file serves both humans (styled document) and parsers (structured data). EFRAG publishes a VSME iXBRL taxonomy; this tool uses it for exports.

Materiality

Decision for each reporting topic: does this actually matter for our business? 'Material' = yes, disclose fully. 'Not material' = no, but you MUST write a basis explaining why. The amended ESRS (Nov 2025) made this active justification mandatory.

Organisational boundary

Which legal entities are inside your report. GHG Protocol offers three consolidation approaches: operational control (activities you manage), financial control (accounting), or equity share (ownership %). Single-entity SMEs almost always pick operational control.

Reduction target

A percentage reduction from a base year to a target year. Best practice: aligned with 1.5°C (science-based), applied to Scope 1+2+3 combined, with a named mechanism. 'Net zero by tomorrow' isn't a target; '-40% by 2027 via trip bundling' is.

RFI — Radiative Forcing Index

Multiplier accounting for the extra warming from aircraft emissions at altitude (non-CO₂ effects: water vapour, NOx, contrails). Roughly 1.3× the ground-level CO₂ impact. Including RFI is the conservative, defensible choice — the 'with RFI' column in DEFRA factors.

Scope 1 — Direct emissions

Emissions from sources you own or control. Burning fossil fuels on site, driving owned vehicles, leaks from owned refrigeration. Memory: what you burn.

Scope 2 — Indirect energy emissions

Emissions from the energy you purchase: electricity, steam, heat, cooling. You don't own the power plant, but you chose to buy from it. Memory: what you buy as energy.

Scope 3.1 — Purchased goods and services

Emissions from producing what you buy: software subscriptions, books, meals, printed materials, consulting services. Often the largest Scope 3 category for service businesses.

Scope 3.2 — Capital goods

Equipment you purchase that lasts longer than a year: laptops, monitors, iPads, furniture. GHG Protocol rule: book the FULL embodied emissions in the year of purchase. Don't amortize over useful life.

Scope 3.6 — Business travel

Flights, trains, ground transport, taxis, personal car when fuel is business-paid. For most knowledge-work businesses this category dominates the footprint, often by 10× or more.

Scope codes

Shorthand identifiers for GHG Protocol categories: '1' (direct), '2-location' / '2-market' (purchased energy, two bases), '3.1' through '3.15' (value chain). Subcategories like '3.1-meals' are internal operational drill-downs that roll up into their parent for reporting.

VSME — Voluntary Standard for non-listed SMEs

Sustainability reporting standard from EFRAG, recommended by the EU Commission (30 July 2025), for SMEs below the CSRD threshold. Two modules: Basic (~50 data points, quick-answer to ESG questionnaires) and Comprehensive (~100 data points, richer narrative).
