Factor library
Emission factor

The number that converts your activity data into kg CO₂e. Each factor has a value, a range, a unit, and a source.

Why we ask: Factor quality is the single biggest driver of your number's credibility. A-rated factors (peer-reviewed / government) are preferred; D-rated (judgment) are flagged for upgrade.

Where to find it: The library under Factors — filter by unit, quality, or geography.

Example: `flight-longhaul-economy-rfi` — DEFRA 2024, 0.196 kg CO₂e/pkm [A].

31 factors · 19 sources · every factor has a quality rating (A = peer-reviewed → D = judgment). Hover any column header for what it means.

Slug
Factor slug

Stable identifier for the emission factor across versions.

Why we ask: Reports cite the slug, so slugs are treated as permanent.

Where to find it: Visible in every row that uses the factor.

Example: `flight-longhaul-economy-rfi`

Value
Mean value

The central estimate — the number we multiply by your quantity.

Why we ask: This is what populates the 'central' column in every report row.

Where to find it: The factor source (DEFRA, SEEG, Apple LCA, etc.).

Example: 0.196 kgCO₂e per passenger-km.

Range
Low–high range

The 5th and 95th percentile bounds around the mean.

Why we ask: Every emission number carries uncertainty. Showing a range is honesty.

Where to find it: The factor's source usually publishes bounds.

Example: DEFRA long-haul economy with RFI: mean 0.196, range 0.18–0.215 kg/pkm.

Unit
Applies-to unit

The unit your quantity must be in when you use this factor.

Why we ask: The tool blocks you if you try to multiply passenger-km by a per-kWh factor. This is the safety check.

Where to find it: Always visible in the factor row.

Example: flight-longhaul-economy-rfi applies to `pkm` (passenger-kilometres).

Geo
Geographic scope

Where the factor is valid (country, region, global).

Why we ask: Grid factors are wildly regional — NL vs BR differ by 5×.

Where to find it: The factor source.

Example: br-grid-location applies to Brazil only.

Year
Valid-from year

The earliest reporting year this factor version is meant for. Annual factors (grid, DEFRA) get a new row each year.

Why we ask: Past reports stay reproducible — their factors are frozen at the version valid in that year.

Where to find it: The factor source's release year.

Example: DEFRA 2024 factors are valid_from_year=2024; they apply to 2024 and 2025 reports.

Quality
Quality A — peer-reviewed or government

The highest data-quality tier. Official government or peer-reviewed science.

Why we ask: A-rated factors are audit-ready. Prefer them wherever possible.

Where to find it: DEFRA, IEA, CBS, IPCC, peer-reviewed literature.

Example: DEFRA 2024 flight factors, CBS NL grid factors.

Used
Used count

How many emission rows in this year reference this factor.

Why we ask: Tells you which factors drive most of your number — where the audit scrutiny will land.

Where to find it: Auto-computed.

Example: 2 uses = both GRU-AMS flights.

Source
Factor source

The publisher of the factor. Always cited with URL when available.

Why we ask: Your report's footnote trail. Readers need to verify.

Where to find it: The Factors / sources subtable.

Example: DEFRA 2024 Conversion Factors (UK DESNZ).
