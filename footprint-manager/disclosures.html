Management summary
Management summary

The 3–5 paragraph executive opener at the top of your report. What a bank loan officer, procurement analyst, or journalist reads first — and often the only thing they read.

Why we ask: Sustainability data is easy to skim past. A management summary in plain language converts your inventory into a story: total, driver, target, methodology, materiality. VSME Comprehensive and CSRD E1 both expect it.

Where to find it: You write it yourself. Markdown (headings/paragraphs/lists/bold/links) is supported.

Example: Typical shape: ¶1 headline total + range; ¶2 where it comes from (dominant scope); ¶3 target + mechanism; ¶4 standard + material topics; ¶5 methodology / audit trail.

1 · Intensity denominators (3 entered)
Intensity denominators

Divide your total emissions by a denominator (revenue, FTE, m²) to get an intensity ratio. These make companies comparable at different scales.

Why we ask: VSME Basic requires kg CO₂e per € turnover. Banks love this metric.

Where to find it: Audited accounts for revenue; payroll for FTE; lease for m².

Example: 8.61 t ÷ €45k = 0.191 kg CO₂e / € turnover.

Total energy you used (in MWh) + how much of it was renewable. Covers electricity, heating, fuel — any energy source.

Why we ask: ESG questionnaires always ask. Also ties back to your Scope 2 rows as a sanity check.

4 · Materiality assessment (4/8 topics)
Materiality assessment

For each VSME topic, decide 'material' (yes, this matters to us) or 'not material' (no, not for our business) — with a written reason.

Why we ask: The amended ESRS requires **active** written justification for non-material claims. VSME permits it. 'Not material' without a basis fails review.

Where to find it: Your operational reality — water stress? office near a Natura 2000 site? any of this relevant?

Example: Water, for a 3 m² home office: not material. Basis: 'Zero process water use; standard drinking-tap only; operator cohort N<3.'

5 · Workforce (VSME Basic — 1 metrics)
Workforce

Headcount, gender ratio, contract types, wages vs minimum, training hours, workplace incidents.

Why we ask: VSME Basic requires workforce metrics even at N=1. GDPR rule: redact anything where a single person is re-identifiable (any cohort <3).

Where to find it: Payroll, HR records.

Reduction targets

A percentage reduction commitment from a base year to a target year.

Why we ask: VSME Comprehensive + ESRS E1-4 expect a structured target. Paris alignment means 1.5°C pathway.

Where to find it: Your strategic plan / leadership's decarbonisation commitment.

Transition plan section
Transition plan

A narrative explaining how you'll hit your reduction target.

Why we ask: ESRS E1-1 asks for it; sceptical stakeholders want to see the mechanism.

Where to find it: Write it in your own words — not a template response.

Example: 'Flights dominate the footprint (7.7 t, ~90%). Lever 1: trip bundling. Lever 2: 2026 emigration which reverses intercontinental frequency.'

Carbon credit (registered, not netted)
Carbon credits

Tonnes of credits you've bought and whether you've retired them.

Why we ask: ESRS E1-7 wants these **disclosed separately, not netted from Scope totals** — that's the important rule. Credits ≠ reductions.

Where to find it: Your registry account (Verra, Gold Standard, UNFCCC CDM).

Example: Zero credits retired in 2025 — the empty row is the disclosure.

Physical and transition climate risks: what could hit your P&L over short, medium, and long horizons.
