Management summary

In 2025, Regen Studio BV emitted approximately 8.6 tonnes CO₂e (range 7.7–9.7 t). The headline number is structurally small for a one-person consulting business, but the distribution tells the more important story: ~95% of our footprint comes from two long-haul flights between São Paulo and Amsterdam. Office electricity, capital goods, meals, SaaS subscriptions, and printed materials combined account for the remaining 5%.

This concentration makes the reduction lever unambiguous. We have committed to a −40% reduction by 2027 against the 2025 baseline, delivered primarily through trip consolidation and the planned 2026 emigration from the Netherlands to Brazil, which flips the direction of travel and reduces annual frequency. No carbon credits were retired in 2025; any future offsetting will be disclosed separately from Scope 1/2/3 totals, consistent with GHG Protocol convention.

The report is structured in alignment with EFRAG Voluntary Standard for non-listed SMEs (VSME) — Basic and Comprehensive modules — and uses the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard with dual Scope 2 disclosure (location-based and market-based). Regen Studio BV is below the post-Omnibus CSRD thresholds and is therefore not obligated under that directive; this disclosure is voluntary, produced to answer counterparty ESG questionnaires and to lead by example for the Digital Product Passport consultancy work we deliver with clients.

Material topics for 2025: climate change (driven by flights). Water, biodiversity, waste, and workforce topics are marked not material with written bases appropriate to a one-FTE home-office operation. An internal carbon price of € 80 per tonne CO₂e is applied to investment decisions from 2026 onward.

Full methodology, factor provenance with quality ratings, and the underlying 34-row activity register are included below. Every emission value has a calculation-run hash for reproducibility, and the HTML version of this report is inline-XBRL tagged per the EFRAG VSME taxonomy for machine readability.

Method in brief. Reporting entity: Regen Studio BV (regen-studio-bv), organisational boundary control. Period: full calendar year 2025. Standard: GHG Protocol Corporate Standard, Scope 1/2/3. Every row carries a 5th–95th percentile range derived from the factor uncertainty; totals propagate the per-row ranges by summation. Offsets, Renewable Energy Certificates, and SAF-allocated reductions are tracked separately and not netted out of the Scope totals shown here. Dual Scope 2: both location-based and market-based figures are disclosed; the grand total uses the market-based value per GHG Protocol convention. Brazil has no formal AIB residual-mix system, so the market-based figure for BR falls back to the location-based grid average. All CO₂-equivalent values use IPCC AR6 100-year GWPs. Where specific receipt dates were not retrieved, entries sit at a within-period reconstruction date — this does not affect quantities. Train (NL) and Uber (BR) are reported as a single annual row each, summed from actual business transactions rather than per-trip detail. The dashboard refuses to record a single row where upper/lower > 100 — a built-in uncertainty guard that flags inputs off by orders of magnitude before they enter the ledger.
